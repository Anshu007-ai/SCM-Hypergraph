This dataset is the primary training source. The 180K+ transactions are processed into a hypergraph where:

6. DATA CONSOLIDATION FOR TRAINING

How Data is Merged for Model Training

The 4 datasets are not concatenated (they have different structures). Instead, they are processed in parallel and combined at the hypergraph level:

StepOperationInputOutput
1. Normalize Min-Max scale each dataset to [0,1] 4 separate CSVs 4 normalized arrays
2. Extract Graph Identify entities (nodes) and interactions (hyperedges) Normalized arrays Incidence matrices H (4 hypergraphs)
3. Generate Labels Compute HCI risk scores from hypergraph structure 4 hypergraphs 4 risk label vectors
4. Create Tensors Convert to PyTorch tensors on GPU Arrays + matrices PyTorch tensors (N, F), (N, M)
5. Train on All Train single model on sequential minibatches from all 4 4 tensor sets One unified model (transfer learning)

Feature Consolidation

The model operates on a unified feature space created by padding/normalizing features across datasets:

Feature Groups (aggregated):
• Economic: Cost, sales, profit, revenue (from DataCo + BOM + IndiGo)
• Operational: Lead time, delivery time, delay, OTP (from DataCo + Maintenance + IndiGo)
• Reliability: Failure rate, tool wear, maintenance incidents (from Maintenance)
• Capacity: Quantity, throughput, aircraft seats (from BOM + IndiGo)
• Temporal: Timestamps, seasonality (from all datasets)

Result: All nodes encoded as (N, F) where F ≈ 10–15 aggregated features
